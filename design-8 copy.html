Youth Groups Lead Environmental Initiative in Mumbai

PS
Reported by BINS
· January 11, 2026 · 5 min read · Mumbai, India

Bahá'í youth across Mumbai have launched an inspiring community-driven environmental project that is transforming neighbourhoods and inspiring residents to take action for a more sustainable future — one tree, one neighbourhood at a time.

Over the past three months, more than 200 young people have come together to plant over 500 trees across various communities in Mumbai. The initiative, which began as a small project in a single neighbourhood, has grown into a citywide movement that is making a tangible difference in the urban landscape.

Volunteers from diverse backgrounds — students, working professionals, and homemakers — have contributed their weekends and evenings to what many describe as a profoundly meaningful experience. Local residents have responded with enthusiasm, with some neighbourhoods even competing to see who can sustain the highest rate of tree survival.

A Vision for Sustainable Communities

The project emerged from study circles and youth gatherings where participants explored the relationship between spiritual principles and environmental stewardship. Inspired by Bahá'í teachings on the unity of humanity and our collective responsibility to care for the earth, the young people decided to translate these ideals into concrete, visible action.

"Building a better world isn't just about grand gestures. It starts with service in our own neighbourhoods — working alongside our neighbours to create spaces that are healthier and more beautiful for everyone."

— Priya Sharma, Youth Coordinator, Mumbai

The group has adopted a disciplined approach, identifying sites with the greatest need, consulting with urban ecologists on species selection, and working with municipal authorities to secure permissions. This systematic process has helped the initiative move from a good intention to a replicable model.

Community Participation and Impact

What makes this initiative particularly remarkable is its inclusive approach. The youth have actively engaged residents from all backgrounds, organising regular cleanup drives, tree-planting events, and educational workshops on sustainable living practices. No prior experience is required to join — just a willingness to serve.

Local schools have also joined the effort, with students participating in weekend activities and learning about environmental conservation. The project has created opportunities for people of different ages and backgrounds to work together toward a common goal, strengthening the bonds of community in the process.

A Model for Other Cities

News of the Mumbai project has spread through Bahá'í communities across India. Similar initiatives have now taken root in Pune, Hyderabad, and Bangalore, each adapted to the specific needs and character of their local environments. Organisers from these cities visited Mumbai in December to learn from the group's experience firsthand.

As the initiative continues to grow, the youth are planning to expand their efforts to include community gardens, waste reduction programs, and educational campaigns about water conservation. Their success demonstrates the power of collective action guided by a commitment to service and a vision of human prosperity rooted in spiritual principles.
