Youth Groups Lead Environmental Initiative in Mumbai

January 11, 2026·5 min read

Bahá'í youth across Mumbai have launched an inspiring community-driven environmental project that is transforming neighbourhoods and inspiring residents to take action for a more sustainable future.

Over the past three months, more than 200 young people have come together to plant over 500 trees across various communities in Mumbai. The initiative, which began as a small project in a single neighbourhood, has grown into a citywide movement making a tangible difference in the urban landscape.

A Vision for Sustainable Communities

The project emerged from study circles and youth gatherings where participants explored the relationship between spiritual principles and environmental stewardship. Inspired by Bahá'í teachings on the unity of humanity and our responsibility to care for the earth, the young people decided to translate these ideals into concrete action.

"We realised that building a better world isn't just about grand gestures," explains Priya Sharma, one of the youth coordinators. "It starts with service in our own neighbourhoods, working alongside our neighbours to create spaces that are healthier and more beautiful for everyone."

Community Participation and Impact

What makes this initiative particularly remarkable is its inclusive approach. The youth have actively engaged residents from all backgrounds, organising regular cleanup drives, tree-planting events, and educational workshops on sustainable living practices.

Local schools have also joined the effort, with students participating in weekend activities and learning about environmental conservation. The project has created opportunities for people of different ages and backgrounds to work together toward a common goal.

Looking Ahead

As the initiative continues to grow, the youth are planning to expand their efforts to include community gardens, waste reduction programs, and educational campaigns about water conservation. Their success has inspired similar projects in other cities across India.
